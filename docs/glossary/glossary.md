# Platform Glossary

> **Document Type:** Glossary
> **Status:** Living Document
> **Owner:** Platform Architecture Team
> **Last Updated:** 2026-05-30

---

This glossary defines terms used throughout the AI Operating Platform blueprint. When terms have ambiguous meanings in different contexts (e.g., "model"), this document specifies the platform-specific meaning.

---

## A

**Agent**
An autonomous AI system deployed on the platform that can plan, reason, and take actions to achieve a goal. Agents have identities, declared capabilities, resource budgets, and are governed by platform policies. Not to be confused with a simple chatbot or LLM wrapper.

**Agent Identity**
A cryptographic identity (X.509 certificate) issued by the platform's Vault PKI to each registered agent. Used to authenticate the agent when calling MCP tools or other platform services.

**Agent Runtime**
Plane 06 of the platform. The execution environment for AI agents, providing lifecycle management, LangGraph orchestration, state management, and governance enforcement.

**Agentic RAG**
A RAG pattern where an AI agent iteratively decides which retrieval queries to run, evaluates result sufficiency, and reformulates queries if needed. Enables multi-hop reasoning over knowledge sources.

**Audit Record**
An immutable, cryptographically signed record of a platform operation. Stored in the Governance Plane's audit log. Cannot be modified or deleted after creation.

---

## C

**Capability Scope**
The declared list of operations an agent is authorized to perform. Agents cannot perform operations outside their capability scope, enforced at the MCP tool authorization layer.

**Checkpointing**
The process of saving agent execution state to Kafka after each step. Enables fault-tolerant long-running agents that can resume from the last checkpoint after a failure.

**Circuit Breaker**
A resilience pattern used in the Model Plane. When a provider fails repeatedly, the circuit breaker "opens" and routes requests to fallback providers, preventing cascade failures.

**Controlled Vocabulary**
A curated list of approved terms and their definitions for a specific domain. Managed by the Semantic Plane to ensure consistent terminology across AI operations.

**Control Plane**
Plane 16 of the platform. The operator interface for managing the platform itself: tenant lifecycle, configuration, upgrades, capacity, and disaster recovery. Not to be confused with a Kubernetes control plane.

---

## D

**Data Lineage**
The record of where data came from, how it was transformed, and where it went. In the AI context, lineage traces from source document through processing, chunking, embedding, retrieval, and model invocation to the final AI output.

**Data Plane**
Plane 02 of the platform. Responsible for data ingestion, transformation, PII detection, masking, and sovereignty enforcement.

**Data Sovereignty**
The principle that data must remain under the legal and geographic control of the organization that owns it. The platform enforces data sovereignty through classification, geographic routing, and cross-border transfer controls.

**Decision Record**
A complete, immutable record of an AI-influenced decision including: input data references, model used, reasoning steps, outcome, confidence, and human review status. Required for regulatory compliance and consumer contestation.

**Default-Deny**
The network security posture where all network traffic between namespaces is blocked unless explicitly allowed. The platform applies default-deny NetworkPolicies to all tenant namespaces.

**Dynamic Secret**
A short-lived credential generated on-demand by HashiCorp Vault (e.g., a 1-hour PostgreSQL username/password). Eliminates long-lived static credentials. Used for all database access on the platform.

---

## E

**Embedding**
A numerical vector representation of text (or other content) that captures semantic meaning. Used in Qdrant for semantic similarity search. Embeddings are generated by the Embedding Service in the Data Plane.

**Embedding Drift**
A change in the distribution of embedding vectors over time, indicating that the semantic representation of knowledge has shifted. Detected by the Evaluation Plane's drift detector.

**Evaluation Plane**
Plane 10 of the platform. Continuously measures AI quality through LLM-as-judge evaluation, RAGAS metrics, and semantic drift detection. Operates quality gates for production promotion.

---

## F

**Fairness Metric**
A quantitative measure of whether an AI system treats different groups of individuals equitably. Common metrics include demographic parity, equalized odds, and individual fairness. Tracked by the Trust Plane.

**Faithfulness**
A RAG evaluation metric measuring whether the AI's answer is grounded in the retrieved context (not hallucinated). Target: > 0.90. Measured by RAGAS and LLM-as-judge.

**Fallback Chain**
An ordered list of AI model providers that the Model Plane tries in sequence when the primary provider fails. Enables resilience against provider outages.

---

## G

**GitOps**
A deployment approach where the desired state of the system is declared in Git, and an operator (Argo CD) continuously reconciles the actual state to match the declared state. All platform deployments use GitOps.

**Governance Plane**
Plane 12 of the platform. Manages policies, immutable audit logs, data lineage, decision records, and compliance report generation.

**GraphRAG**
A RAG pattern that combines vector similarity search with knowledge graph traversal. Enables multi-hop reasoning and relationship-aware context retrieval. Implemented using Neo4j + Qdrant.

---

## H

**HITL (Human-in-the-Loop)**
A pattern where an AI agent pauses execution and requests human input before continuing. Used for high-stakes decisions, low-confidence scenarios, or regulatory requirements. Managed by the Agent Runtime's HITL Manager.

**Hybrid RAG**
A RAG pattern combining dense vector search (semantic similarity) with sparse keyword search (BM25). The results are fused using Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF). Outperforms pure vector search for domain-specific terminology.

---

## K

**Knowledge Graph**
A structured representation of entities and their relationships, stored in Neo4j. Enables semantic reasoning over organizational knowledge. The platform's Knowledge Plane manages knowledge graphs.

**Knowledge Plane**
Plane 03 of the platform. Extracts entities from documents, resolves duplicate entities, maps relationships, and stores them in a knowledge graph for AI retrieval.

---

## L

**LangGraph**
The agent orchestration framework used by the platform. Enables graph-based, stateful agent execution with human-in-the-loop support and pluggable checkpointers.

**Lineage ID**
A platform-assigned identifier that links a data entity (document, chunk, entity) to its complete provenance record in the lineage service.

---

## M

**MCP (Model Context Protocol)**
An open protocol (initiated by Anthropic) for standardizing how AI agents interact with external tools, resources, and data sources. The platform uses MCP for all agent tool integration.

**MCP Server**
A service that exposes functionality to AI agents via the Model Context Protocol. Platform MCP servers include: knowledge-graph, vector-search, document-retrieval, human-review.

**Model Plane**
Plane 05 of the platform. The provider-agnostic AI model routing layer. All model invocations go through the Model Plane, which handles provider selection, fallback, rate limiting, cost tracking, and governance hooks.

**Model Registry**
A catalog of registered AI models including their provider, version, capabilities, cost, and routing rules. Part of the Registry Plane.

**Multi-Tenancy**
The ability to serve multiple organizations (tenants) from shared infrastructure while maintaining strict data isolation, resource fairness, and independent configuration.

---

## N

**Namespace (Kubernetes)**
A virtual cluster partition within Kubernetes. The platform allocates one namespace per tenant for compute isolation. Combined with NetworkPolicies, it provides strong tenant isolation.

**Namespace (Vault)**
A Vault Enterprise feature providing isolated secret stores per tenant within a shared Vault cluster.

---

## O

**Observability Plane**
Plane 14 of the platform. Collects and surfaces traces, metrics, and logs using OpenTelemetry, Prometheus, Loki, Jaeger, and Grafana.

**Ontology**
A formal representation of knowledge as a set of concepts and their relationships. In the AI platform context, ontologies define the meaning of business terms (e.g., "Customer", "Policy", "Claim") in a machine-readable way. Managed by the Semantic Plane.

**OPA (Open Policy Agent)**
An open-source policy engine using the Rego language. Used for authorization decisions across all platform services and governance policy evaluation.

**OTEL (OpenTelemetry)**
An open standard for distributed tracing, metrics, and logs. All platform services are instrumented with OpenTelemetry. Telemetry is vendor-neutral and exportable to any compatible backend.

---

## P

**PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**
Data that can identify an individual (name, email, account number, SSN, etc.). The platform detects PII using Microsoft Presidio and applies masking before AI model access.

**Platform Foundation**
Plane 01 of the platform. The infrastructure substrate: Kubernetes, Vault, Argo CD, cert-manager, networking, and autoscaling.

**Policy**
A declarative rule that governs platform behavior. Policies are written in Rego (OPA), version-controlled in Git, and evaluated in real-time by the Governance Plane.

**Provider Adapter**
A class implementing the ModelAdapter interface for a specific AI provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, Bedrock, etc.). All adapters expose the same interface to the Model Plane.

**Prompt Injection**
An attack where malicious instructions are embedded in user input or retrieved content to manipulate an AI model's behavior. The Security Plane's Prompt Injection Detector defends against this.

---

## Q

**Quality Gate**
An automated check in the Evaluation Plane that blocks the promotion of an AI artifact (agent, model configuration, prompt) to production if it fails quality thresholds.

---

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**
A technique where relevant knowledge is retrieved at query time and provided as context to a language model, grounding its response in current, authoritative information.

**RAGAS**
An evaluation framework for measuring RAG pipeline quality. Metrics include: faithfulness, answer relevancy, context recall, context precision.

**Reranker**
A cross-encoder model that scores candidate retrieved chunks against a query. Used after initial ANN retrieval to improve precision. Significantly improves RAG quality.

**Registry Plane**
Plane 09 of the platform. The central catalog for all platform artifacts: models, agents, tools, prompts, skills, evaluation datasets, and workflow definitions.

**Rego**
The policy language used by Open Policy Agent (OPA). Platform governance policies are written in Rego.

---

## S

**Semantic Plane**
Plane 04 of the platform. Manages ontologies, taxonomies, and controlled vocabularies. Provides the shared meaning layer for all AI operations.

**Service Mesh**
An infrastructure layer providing service-to-service communication features including mTLS, traffic management, and observability. Istio or Linkerd.

**Sovereign Deployment**
A deployment where all data processing occurs on-premises or in a specific geographic region, with no data leaving that boundary. The platform supports sovereign deployment via on-premises Kubernetes + Ollama.

**SPIFFE/SPIRE**
A standards-based framework for service identity in distributed systems. Optional complement to Vault PKI for service identity management.

---

## T

**Tenant**
An organizational unit that uses the platform. A tenant may be a business unit, subsidiary, external client, or department. Each tenant has isolated data, configuration, and resources.

**Token Budget**
The maximum number of tokens an agent is permitted to consume in a single run. Enforced by the Agent Runtime to prevent runaway AI costs.

**Trust Plane**
Plane 13 of the platform. Tracks fairness metrics, generates explainability artifacts, manages human oversight requirements, and computes trust scores for AI systems.

**Trust Score**
A composite score (0-1) representing the overall trustworthiness of an AI agent based on fairness, calibration, explainability, oversight compliance, and consistency metrics.

---

## V

**Vector**
A numerical array (embedding) representing semantic content. Stored in Qdrant and used for similarity search.

**Vault (HashiCorp)**
The platform's secrets management system. Provides dynamic secrets, PKI, transit encryption, and multi-tenant secret isolation. All platform credentials are managed through Vault.

---

## W

**Working Memory**
Short-lived agent state stored in Redis during an agent run. Includes the current conversation context, intermediate results, and tool outputs. Expires after the run completes.

**Workflow Orchestration**
Plane 08 of the platform. Manages multi-step, multi-actor business processes that combine AI agents, human tasks, external system calls, and conditional logic.

---

## Z

**Zero-Trust**
A security model where no actor (user, service, agent) is trusted by default. Every request must be authenticated and authorized, regardless of network location.
